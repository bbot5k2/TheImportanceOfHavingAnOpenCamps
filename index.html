The Importance of Having an Open Campus

A public service announcement on student freedom, satisfaction, and school experience.

The importance of having an open campus is based on the idea that allowing students more freedom can improve their overall school experience. Having an open campus can increase student satisfaction with school. Surveys often ask students about their happiness, stress levels, and overall school experience, and schools with open campuses report higher student satisfaction scores compared to closed-campus schools. A study published in Economics of Education Review studied open and closed campus policies in high schools by viewing academic performance and attendance data. The study found that “when open campus privileges were offered as an incentive, the study found measurable differences in student academic and attendance outcomes compared to schools without open campus policies.” This shows that having an open campus can increase grades, and to motivate students to attend school. Not having an open campus is an issue. This is because of increased stress and frustration, reduced independence, and fewer opportunities for responsibility, for example coming back on time. This argument appeals to ethos through credible academic research, logos through data and measurable outcomes, and pathos by highlighting how closed campus policies can increase student stress and reduce independence.
